Correlations Discovered Between Healthcare, Age, and Income

The first scatter plot shows that there is a positive correlation between poverty levels and lack of healthcare. States with higher percentages of people living in poverty tend to have a larger percentage of residents that do not have healthcare.

When comparing median age to lack of healthcare, there does not appear to be a correlation. Most states have median ages between 36-42, forming a cluster without a significant trend.

Household income and lack of healthcare appear to have a negative correlation. As median income increases, the lack of healthcare in a state decreases. This makes sense given that there is already an observed positive correlation between poverty levels and lack of healthcare.
